## **2.1 Temporal Regime Determines Geometry**

General relativity deepened this. In curved spacetime, there is no global time. Different observers at different positions in a gravitational field experience different proper times. The Schwarzschild metric makes this explicit: as an observer approaches a black hole horizon, their proper time diverges from the coordinate time of a distant observer. The distant observer never sees the infalling observer cross the horizon. The infalling observer crosses in finite proper time. Both descriptions are correct. They correspond to different temporal regimes.
